Best PLA Filament for 3D Printing in 2026

Updated March 2026 · 8 min read · By Layered Up

If you're into 3D printing, you know that filament choice can make or break a print. After running hundreds of spools through our Bambu Lab A1 over the past year, we've narrowed down the absolute best PLA filaments you can buy right now. Whether you're a beginner looking for reliability or a seasoned maker chasing that perfect surface finish, this list has you covered.

What Makes a Great PLA Filament?

Before we dive into the picks, here's what we look for when testing filament:

  • Consistency: Does every spool print the same? No clogs, no diameter variations.
  • Surface finish: How smooth and glossy does the final print look?
  • Bed adhesion: Does it stick without a raft or excessive glue?
  • Color accuracy: Does the filament match what's shown on the listing?
  • Value: Price per kilogram relative to quality.

We test at standard settings — 210°C nozzle, 60°C bed, 50mm/s on a textured PEI plate. Let's get into it.

Our Top PLA Filament Picks

If you own a Bambu printer, this is the obvious choice — but honestly, it's fantastic on any printer. Bambu's PLA Basic has the tightest diameter tolerance we've seen (±0.02mm), and it prints like butter. The color range has expanded massively, and every spool we've used has been flawless.

✅ Pros

  • Extremely consistent diameter
  • Excellent layer adhesion
  • Huge color selection
  • Works perfectly with AMS

❌ Cons

  • Slightly pricier than budget options
  • Only available in 1kg spools

Best for: Bambu printer owners, anyone wanting zero-hassle reliability.

🥈 2. Hatchbox PLA — Best for Beginners

Hatchbox has been the community favorite for years, and for good reason. It's forgiving, widely available, and prints consistently across nearly every printer on the market. If you're just starting out and don't want to overthink your filament choice, grab Hatchbox and start printing.

  • Forgiving print settings

  • Vacuum-sealed packaging

❌ Cons

  • Color selection is more limited
  • Surface finish not as glossy as premium options

Best for: Beginners, Ender 3 users, anyone who wants reliability without a premium price tag.

🥉 3. eSUN PLA+ — Best Strength

Regular PLA is great, but it's brittle. eSUN's PLA+ solves that with a modified formula that's noticeably tougher without sacrificing printability. We use this for functional prints — phone mounts, clips, brackets — anything that needs to take some stress without snapping.

✅ Pros

  • Significantly stronger than standard PLA
  • Prints at PLA temperatures
  • Great layer adhesion
  • Affordable price point

❌ Cons

  • Slightly more stringing than standard PLA
  • Can warp on large flat prints

Best for: Functional prints, mechanical parts, anything that needs to be tough.

4. Overture PLA — Best Budget Pick

Overture consistently punches above its weight class. At often under $20 per kilogram, it's one of the cheapest PLAs on Amazon — but it doesn't print like cheap filament. We've used it for large batch prints where we're going through multiple spools, and it holds up surprisingly well.

✅ Pros

  • Incredible value — often under $20/kg
  • Comes with build surface sample
  • Decent color range
  • Resealable packaging

❌ Cons

  • Occasional diameter inconsistency
  • Some colors print differently than expected

Best for: Budget-conscious makers, large batch prints, prototyping.

When you want a print that looks like it was injection-molded in metal, silk PLA is the move. SUNLU's version gives you that gorgeous pearlescent shimmer without needing any post-processing. We use it for display pieces, gifts, and anything where visual impact matters more than raw strength.

  • Multiple metallic colors available

❌ Cons
